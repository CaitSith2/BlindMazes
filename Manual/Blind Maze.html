On the Subject of Blind Maze

Not only do you have to find the exit, you have to find the beginning!

This Module has four buttons labeled N for North, E for East, S for South, and W for West. The Defuser must use the keys to navigate a maze they cannot see. Use the following steps to discover your current maze, where you are in the maze, and which side of the maze the exit is on.


STEP 1: Maze Type
  • Take the last digit of the Serial Number.
  • Add the number of solved modules.
  • If the number is above 9, subtract 10 until it is between 0 and 9.
  • Locate the maze below with the same number as your final result this is your maze, however it may be rotated.

STEP 2: Maze Rotation

Use the first conditional that applies out of the following list to determine what rotation the maze has.

  • If there is exactly one D Battery and no AA Batteries, then rotate the maze 90 degrees clockwise.
  • Otherwise, if there are less than 3 unique ports on the bomb, then rotate the maze 270 degrees clockwise.
  • Otherwise, if there is a lit IND indicator and there is a vowel in the serial number, then rotate the maze 180 degrees.
  • Otherwise, if there are no yellow buttons and at least one red button, then rotate the maze 90 degrees counterclockwise.
  • Otherwise, if there are at least 2 other Maze-Based[1] Modules, then rotate the maze 180 degrees.
  • If there are two or more red buttons, and there is an unlit MSA indicator, then rotate the maze 270 degrees counterclockwise.
  • Otherwise, keep the maze as it is.

STEP 3: Beginning Location

Look at the keys, use Table A, referring both the color of each key and the letter on each key, to find the value of each key.

FOR X:
  • Add the values of the South and North keys together.
  • If the sum is above 5, subtract 5 until the number is between 1 and 5.

FOR Y:
  • Add the values of the East and West keys together.
  • If the sum above 5, subtract 5 until the number is between 1 and 5.

(Note that both X and Y are after rotation, and move Left to Right and Top to Bottom, respectively.)


[1] Registered Maze Based modules are Vanilla Maze, Morse-A-Maze, 3D Maze, Mouse In The Maze, and Heximaze.
